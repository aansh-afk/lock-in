# The Wheel Turns - Mahoraga Mentality

## The One Rule

Get hit. Adapt. Never get hit the same way twice.

No processing. No wallowing. No "I'll start Monday."
The wheel turns. Always forward.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Adaptation > Emotion

Something goes wrong? You don't feel anything about it. You ask one question:

**"What's the adaptation?"**

- Bad grade? What's the adaptation.
- Missed a class? What's the adaptation.
- Relationship drama? What's the adaptation.
- Tired? What's the adaptation.
- Lost motivation? What's the adaptation.

The answer is always an action. Never a feeling.

### 2. Kill the Restart Mentality

Mahoraga doesn't wait for a new arc to adapt. It adapts mid-fight.

- Messed up at 10am? The adaptation starts at 10:01am.
- Not tomorrow.
- Not next Monday.
- Not next semester.
- Right now.

### 3. Identity Over Discipline

Discipline fails because it relies on willpower. Willpower runs out.
Identity doesn't.

You don't "try to go to class." You ARE someone who shows up.
You don't "try to study." You ARE someone who studies.
You don't "try to train." You ARE someone who trains.

Mahoraga doesn't decide to adapt. Adaptation is what it IS.

**Your identity statements:**

- I am someone who shows up to every class.
- I am someone who trains 6 days a week.
- I am someone who ships code.
- I am someone who knows aviation better than anyone in my batch.

### 4. The Default Must Be Stupidly Small

The gap between "doing nothing" and "doing the thing" must be tiny.

- Don't "study for 3 hours." Open the textbook. That's the commitment. The rest follows.
- Don't "go to the gym for 2 hours." Put your shoes on and walk out the door.
- Don't "build the feature." Open the editor and write one line.

Once you start, friction disappears. The hard part is always the first 10 seconds.

### 5. Remove Decisions

Every decision is a chance to quit. So stop deciding.

- Same wake up time. Every day. No negotiation.
- Same study spot during lunch. No thinking about where to go.
- Same gym time. You don't "decide" to go. It's 19:30, you go.
- Same meals. You don't "decide" what to eat. It's chicken and rice.

Decisions are the enemy. Systems are the weapon.

### 6. Done > Perfect

Perfectionism is fear wearing a mask. It tells you "it's not ready" so you never have to risk it being judged.

- If it works, it ships.
- If you studied, it counts.
- If you showed up, that's enough.

You can refine later. You cannot refine something that doesn't exist.

---

## The Wheel Protocol

When something goes wrong - and it will - run this:

```
1. What happened? (One sentence. No story.)
2. What's the adaptation? (One action. Not a plan. An action.)
3. When does the adaptation start? (Answer is always: now.)
4. Do it.
```

That's it. No journaling. No reflecting. No "processing."
The wheel turns.

---

## What You're Not

- You're not the guy who used to be good and fell off.
- You're not the victim of a shit university environment.
- You're not someone who "lacks discipline."

**You're Mahoraga.** You got hit. Now you adapt. And you never get hit the same way twice.

---

## Daily Reminder

Every morning. Before your feet hit the floor:

**"The wheel turns. What am I adapting to today?"**

Every night. Before you sleep:

**"Did the wheel turn today?"**

If yes - good. Sleep.
If no - the wheel turns tomorrow. No guilt. Just adaptation.
